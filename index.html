Who I am

My name is David, I am 30 years old, currently living in Berlin. I will be on a 3 month sabbatical to find a way to get my writing online after finally finishing my formal education mid-September 2024 with the goal of working as a lawyer. I am interested in philosophy, sociology and faith, especially how it manifests in ways that are not classically deemed religious.

I have a background in a varied movement practice, ranging from olympic weight lifting, modern dance and Tai Chi, mainly inspired by folks like @portal.ido and Moshé Feldenkrais, although all of it has been very much on hold since entering the final exams phase. I can't wait to pick up were I've left off.

What I aim at

First, to re-engage with the above. What I liked most about life was the unhinged binge-sessions into random topics, gaining insight and integrating it into the present. Because of the exams, I was not able to do so for a long time and I really feel the toll.

Second, to share the insights of cleaning up the mess of a formal education (see below).

Third, find a scene to start building stuff together.

I have already created some kind of framework to engage with, which looks like this:

  • On X I want to post daily. With the inspiration of @DefenderofBasic, I want to use X like a Slack, but at the same time, I see that @CulturalTutor - Format seems to work really well. So I see something like 100-200 post Atomic Essay or deep dives into the history of things
  • On Medium I want to post weekly. I have found, Medium loves the personal essay mixed with practical insights. 1000-2000 words are the preferred range.
  • On Substack I want to post monthly. Here I can summarize what I have accomplished in the month, paired with insights and book, podcasts or video series recommendations

The aim here is to experiment with different formats and find out what works for me. The overarching aim is to find my way as a freelance writer, able to sustain myself from my writing. I am not sure, how exactly this will work. I have come to the realization, that the doing is the motive, not the end goal. I realize that I can only say this, because I have a formal education, but one has to amor your fati, right?
